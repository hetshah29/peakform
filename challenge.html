PHASE BREAKDOWN

Phase 1: Foundation (Days 1–30)

Learn movement patterns. Build a base of strength and cardiovascular conditioning. Focus entirely on form quality over heavy loading.

  • 4 training days / week
  • 2 cardio sessions / week
  • 1 full rest day
  • Sets: 3×10–12 reps
  • Rest: 60–90 seconds between sets
  • Week 4: Deload & assessment

Phase 2: Intensity (Days 31–60)

Progressive overload intensifies. Volume increases, rest periods decrease. Supersets and circuits are introduced. Real strength gains begin.

  • 5 training days / week
  • HIIT sessions added
  • Sets: 4×8–12 reps
  • Rest: 45–60 seconds
  • Push-Pull-Legs split introduced
  • Drop sets and supersets in Week 8

Phase 3: Peak (Days 61–90)

Maximum intensity. Heavy compound lifts, metabolic conditioning, advanced techniques, and PR attempts. Your hardest and most rewarding phase.

  • 5–6 training days / week
  • Heavy 3–5RM strength work
  • Cluster sets and rest-pause
  • Sprint conditioning added
  • Barbell complex circuits
  • Day 90: Full fitness assessment & PRs
