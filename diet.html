NUTRITION GUIDE

Precision nutrition protocols for muscle gain, fat loss, performance, and lifelong health

NUTRITION FOUNDATIONS

🔥 Total Calories

Energy balance is the master variable. Your total calorie intake determines whether you gain, maintain, or lose weight. Calculate your TDEE and adjust ±300–500 calories based on your goal.

Fat Loss (Deficit)–500 kcal
Muscle Gain (Surplus)+300 kcal

🥩 Protein — Build & Preserve

The building block of muscle. Target 0.8–1g per lb of bodyweight daily. Spread across 4–5 meals for optimal muscle protein synthesis. Prioritize lean meats, eggs, dairy, fish, and legumes.

Goal: 150–200g / day for 180lb athlete

⚡ Carbohydrates — Fuel

Your primary energy source. Time carbs around workouts for maximum performance and recovery. Choose complex carbs: oats, brown rice, sweet potatoes, whole grain bread, quinoa.

Pre-workout: 30–50g fast carbs
Post-workout: 50–80g for glycogen replenishment

🫒 Healthy Fats — Hormones

Critical for testosterone and growth hormone production, joint health, and vitamin absorption. Include avocado, nuts, olive oil, fatty fish, and whole eggs. Never drop below 20% of calories.

Target: 0.4–0.5g per lb of bodyweight

💧 Hydration — The Overlooked Key

Muscle tissue is 75% water. Even 2% dehydration reduces strength and performance significantly. Drink 0.5–1oz per lb of bodyweight. Add electrolytes during sessions longer than 60 minutes.

Minimum: 3–4 liters per day

⏰ Meal Timing

Eat every 3–4 hours to maintain muscle protein synthesis. Pre-workout meal 1–2 hours before training. Post-workout nutrition within 45 minutes. Avoid high-fat meals pre-workout as fat slows digestion.

LEAN BULK PROTOCOL

📈 Caloric Surplus

Aim for +250–400 calories above TDEE. Aggressive bulks lead to excess fat gain. A lean bulk maximizes muscle while minimizing fat accumulation. Target 0.25–0.5 lb weight gain per week.

💪 Protein Priority

1–1.2g per lb of bodyweight. Never sacrifice protein during a bulk. It's the raw material for every gram of new muscle. Distribute across 5–6 daily feedings for optimal synthesis.

🍚 Carb Cycling

Higher carbs on training days (up to 3g/lb). Reduce by 25–30% on rest days. This nutrient timing maximizes glycogen storage for performance while minimizing unnecessary fat gain.

🍳 Best Bulk Foods

Chicken breast, whole eggs, brown rice, oats, sweet potato, avocado, Greek yogurt, cottage cheese, whole milk, pasta, banana, whey protein, lean beef, salmon, quinoa.

📅 Bulk Duration

Run a lean bulk for 12–20 weeks, then transition to a cut. This bulk-cut cycle is the most proven method for building a lean, muscular physique over time. Track progress monthly.

⚡ Peri-Workout Nutrition

Pre: 30–50g carbs + 20g protein, 60–90 min before. Post: 40–60g carbs + 30–40g fast protein within 30–45 minutes. This is your most critical nutrition window of the entire day.

FAT LOSS PROTOCOL

🔻 Caloric Deficit

Create a 300–500 calorie deficit daily. Larger deficits risk muscle loss and hormonal disruption. Prioritize high-protein to preserve lean mass. Target 0.5–1 lb of fat loss per week maximum.

🛡️ Muscle Preservation

Increase protein to 1–1.2g/lb during a cut. Higher-than-usual protein prevents muscle catabolism while in a caloric deficit. Resistance training is equally essential — never stop lifting.

🥦 Volume Eating

Fill your plate with high-volume, low-calorie foods: leafy greens, cucumber, broccoli, cauliflower, zucchini, berries, mushrooms. These keep you full without breaking your calorie budget.

🚫 Foods to Minimize

Liquid calories (juice, alcohol, soda), refined sugars, white bread, ultra-processed snacks, fast food, excessive cooking oils. Calorie-dense with low nutritional value — these sabotage a cut.

🔄 Refeed Days

Every 1–2 weeks: bring calories to maintenance and spike carbs significantly. Replenishes glycogen, boosts leptin, improves mood and performance. Prevents metabolic adaptation to dieting.

💊 Cut Support Stack

Creatine (strength maintenance), caffeine (performance + thermogenesis), protein powder (macro targets), omega-3 (inflammation), Vitamin D + Zinc (hormonal support through deficit).

SUPPLEMENT STACK

🏆 Whey Protein

The gold standard. Fast-absorbing post-workout protein. 25–30g per serving. Whey isolate for higher purity. Essential for hitting daily protein targets efficiently.

⚡ Creatine Monohydrate

Most researched supplement. 5g daily (no loading needed). Increases strength, power output, and muscle cell hydration. Safe for long-term use. Non-negotiable in any stack.

☕ Caffeine

3–6mg/kg bodyweight, 30–60 min pre-workout. Improves focus, strength, and endurance. Best cycled 5 days on / 2 off to maintain sensitivity. Avoid after 2pm.

🐟 Omega-3 Fish Oil

2–3g EPA+DHA daily. Reduces inflammation, supports joint health, improves recovery, aids cardiovascular health. Take with meals to avoid aftertaste.

☀️ Vitamin D3 + K2

5,000 IU D3 + 100mcg K2 daily. Critical for testosterone production, immune function, and calcium metabolism. Most athletes are chronically deficient.

🌙 Magnesium Glycinate

400mg before bed. Improves sleep quality, reduces muscle cramps, supports 300+ enzymatic processes including protein synthesis. Game-changing for recovery.

🧪 Beta-Alanine

3.2–6.4g daily. Buffers lactic acid, extending muscular endurance. Ideal for high-rep training and conditioning work. Tingling (paresthesia) is normal and harmless.

🔬 Zinc

25–45mg daily with food. Directly tied to testosterone levels. Depleted by heavy sweating. Pair with magnesium for maximum hormonal support and recovery enhancement.

🌿 Ashwagandha (KSM-66)

600mg daily. Adaptogen that reduces cortisol, improves recovery, and has been shown to increase testosterone and muscle strength in clinical trials.

💊 Collagen + Vitamin C

10–15g collagen peptides + 500mg Vitamin C before training. Directly supports tendon, ligament, and joint repair. Essential for longevity and injury prevention.
